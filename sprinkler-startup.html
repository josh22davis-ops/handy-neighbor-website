Bring Your Irrigation System Back to Life This Spring

Spring is here, and your sprinkler system needs to be checked after months of winter dormancy. A proper spring startup ensures your system is leak-free, all zones are functioning, and your lawn gets the water it needs. If your system wasn't properly winterized, hidden damage may be waiting to be discovered—and we'll find it before you waste water or damage your landscape.

The Handy Neighbor Co. provides professional spring startup service that activates your system safely, tests each zone, and inspects for leaks and damage. We check valve function, head spray patterns, and water pressure to ensure everything is working as it should. If we find issues, we'll let you know what needs attention so you can make informed decisions about repairs.

Serving West Omaha's finest neighborhoods—The Ridges, Shadow Ridge, Armbrust Acres, Bay Shores, Leawood, and Pacific Park—for over 20 years, we've started thousands of systems. Josh Davis, our licensed and insured owner-operator, knows what a healthy West Omaha irrigation system looks like, and he ensures yours is ready for the growing season.

What's Included in Our Spring Startup Service

  • System pressure check and main valve activation
  • Individual zone testing and activation
  • Sprinkler head inspection and adjustment
  • Leak detection throughout system
  • Valve and backflow device verification
  • Documentation of system status and findings

We activate your system carefully, checking each zone to ensure proper operation. If we find leaks, damaged heads, or valve problems, we'll document them and discuss your options.

Why West Omaha Homeowners Trust Us for Startup Service

Thorough zone testing: We don't just turn on the water and leave. We test each zone individually to ensure proper coverage and water pressure throughout your system.

Early leak detection: Hidden leaks can waste hundreds of gallons and damage landscaping. We find them during startup, not weeks later when your water bill shows the damage.

Spring readiness: A properly started system means your lawn gets consistent water throughout the growing season. We set you up for success.

Local West Omaha knowledge: We understand the soil types, climate, and landscape characteristics of West Omaha neighborhoods. Our startup approach is tailored to what works here.

Transparent findings: We document what we find and explain your options clearly. No pressure to repair or replace—you decide what makes sense.

When to Schedule Your Spring Startup

Timing matters. In West Omaha, we typically recommend scheduling startup in mid-April through early May, after the last frost risk has passed. This gives you a few weeks to address any issues before you really need your irrigation running full time. Earlier is better—don't wait until June when your lawn is already stressed.
