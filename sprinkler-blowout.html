Professional fall winterization to protect your irrigation system from Omaha's freezing temperatures.

Protect Your Irrigation System Before Winter Hits

When temperatures drop below freezing, water trapped in your sprinkler lines and valves expands, causing cracks and breaks that can cost thousands to repair. A fall sprinkler blowout removes all water from your system before the first freeze, preventing winter damage and keeping your irrigation system ready for spring activation.

In West Omaha, where winter temperatures regularly plunge below zero, this single service can save you from expensive repairs and the stress of a non-functional irrigation system come spring. The Handy Neighbor Co. provides professional blowout service using proper equipment and technique to completely evacuate your lines, ensuring nothing is left behind to freeze and damage your system.

Serving The Ridges, Shadow Ridge, Armbrust Acres, Bay Shores, Leawood, and Pacific Park with over 20 years of experience, we've helped hundreds of West Omaha homeowners winterize their systems. Josh Davis, our licensed and insured owner-operator, knows the local weather patterns and what your system needs to survive our winters.

What's Included in Our Sprinkler Blowout

  • Complete system shutdown and winterization procedure
  • Compressed air blowout of all irrigation lines
  • Valve and backflow preventer clearing
  • Drain valve opening to prevent freeze damage
  • System pressure verification throughout
  • Final inspection and documentation

Our blowout ensures every drop of water is removed from your system. We use compressed air at appropriate pressure to flush lines completely, without causing damage.

The Right Time for Sprinkler Blowout

Fall is the critical window for winterization. In West Omaha, we typically recommend scheduling your blowout in late September through October, before the first freeze. Waiting until November or December risks an unexpected cold snap catching your system unprepared. The Handy Neighbor Co. handles scheduling to ensure you're protected before winter arrives.
