The Filter You Bought Is Probably a Dust Collector

If you’re like most homeowners, you grabbed whatever was cheapest at the hardware store, slid it in, and forgot about it. And that’s a problem — because the basic filters most people buy are essentially dust collectors. They keep your HVAC blower from getting gunked up. That’s about it. They’re doing almost nothing for the air your family actually breathes.

A properly rated MERV 13 filter, on the other hand, captures particles down to 0.3 microns — the size range where viruses, bacteria, and other airborne pathogens live. The EPA estimates indoor air can be two to five times more polluted than outdoor air, and your HVAC filter is the single biggest tool you have to do something about that. But only if the filter is up to the job — and only if it gets replaced before it chokes your system.

The catch: higher-rated filters fill up faster, custom HVAC systems take custom sizes, and most homeowners forget which size they bought last time. That’s why we built filter service into every membership.

What "Your Filter, Handled" Actually Means

  • We identify the right MERV rating for your specific HVAC system — not too low to protect, not too high to choke airflow
  • We measure and confirm the exact filter size your unit takes (custom sizes included)
  • We source quality filters — you don’t hunt for them at three different stores
  • We replace them on the right cadence and check them at every seasonal visit
  • We document every change in your home’s maintenance record so there’s no guessing about when the last one happened

That’s the difference between buying a filter and having filter service. One is a Saturday errand. The other is a system that runs itself.
